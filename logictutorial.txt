# FILL GAME DART: COMPLETE LOGIC TUTORIAL

Welcome to the official logic guide for Fill Game Dart. This tutorial covers everything from basic hits to advanced batch strategies and bonuses.

---

## 1. THE GAME STRUCTURE (BATCHES)

The game is played in two distinct phases called "Batches".

### BATCH 1: THE QUALIFICATION (221.5 PTS)
- **Goal**: Be the first to reach or exceed the target score of **221.5 points**.
- **The Bar**: Once a player hits 221.5, Batch 1 ends. Their final score becomes "The Bar" that the opponent must try to beat in Batch 2.

### BATCH 2: THE FINAL RACE (BEAT THE BAR)
- **Goal**: Reach a score higher than your opponent's final Batch 1 score.
- **Victory**: The first player to surpass their specific target wins the entire game immediately.

---

## 2. THE DARTBOARD TARGETS

### DIRECT NUMBER HITS (1-14)
- You can target numbers 1 through 14 directly.
- Each hit progresses your "Personal Completion" for that number (e.g., Number 10 requires 10 hits to complete).

### RING HITS (GROUPS)
Hitting a ring is a powerful move that awards hits to **ALL** numbers in that group:
- **Ring 1 (Inner)**: 14, 13
- **Ring 2**: 12, 9, 5, 10
- **Ring 3**: 11, 1, 3, 8
- **Ring 4 (Outer)**: 7, 4, 2, 6

---

## 3. THE SCORING SYSTEM

### A. FILLER POINTS (+2 PER HIT)
- **Numbers 2-14**: Every hit grants +2 pts, capped at `(Number Value * 2)`.
- **Number 1 SPECIAL**: Awarded **ONLY ONCE per batch**. Your first hit on #1 in Batch 1 gives pts; subsequent hits in Batch 1 give 0 filler pts. The same applies to Batch 2.

### B. TOP FILLER BONUS (+7 PER NUMBER)
- **Applicable**: Numbers 2 - 14.
- **Condition**: Awarded once **BOTH** players have hit more than half of the number's requirement.
- **Winner**: The player with the most hits on that number gets +7 pts.
- **Tie**: Both players get +3.5 pts each.

### C. FILL-UP BONUS (+10 PER NUMBER)
- **Number 1 SPECIAL**: Awarded **ONLY ONCE per batch**. Your first hit on #1 in Batch 1 gives +10 pts; subsequent hits in Batch 1 give 0. The same applies to Batch 2.
- **Numbers 2-14**: Awarded to the player who "closes" the number for the whole board (completes it for themselves after the opponent has already finished).

---

## 4. NUMBER 1 SUMMARY: THE "12-POINT RULE"
- **Batch 1 (First Hit)**: Grants **+12 total points** (2 Filler + 10 Fill-Up).
- **Batch 1 (Later Hits)**: Grants **0 points**.
- **Batch 2 (First Hit)**: Grants **+12 total points**.
- **Batch 2 (Later Hits)**: Grants **0 points**.

---

## 5. EXAMPLE SCENARIO: A RING 3 STRIKE (BATCH 1)

If you hit **Ring 3** (Numbers 11, 1, 3, 8):
1. **Hits Recorded**: You get +1 hit on all numbers.
2. **Scoring for #1**: If this is your first hit on #1 in Batch 1, you get **+12 pts**.
3. **Scoring for Others**: You get +2 pts for #11, +2 pts for #3, and +2 pts for #8 (assuming they aren't capped).
4. **Total**: 12 + 2 + 2 + 2 = **18 points** from one ring hit!

---

## 6. PRO TIPS
- **Don't Waste Darts on #1**: Since it only pays out once per batch, don't keep aiming for it (directly or via Ring 3) once you've collected your 12 points.
- **Batch 1 Strategy**: Aim for high numbers to build a massive "Bar" that your opponent will struggle to beat in Batch 2.

## 7. DART HIT CALCULATION

### FOR HUMAN PLAYERS (RANDOMIZED RESOLUTION)
When you click to throw, the game calculates your landing coordinates `(lx, ly)` using:
- **Target Choice**: 70% chance to aim for a specific Number Badge, 30% to aim for a Ring Line.
- **Coordinate Generation**:
  - Hits are calculated using `polarToXY(angle, radius)`.
  - **Number Hit**: Centers on the mid-angle of the number's segment and its specific ring radius.
  - **Ring Hit**: Hits the exact boundary line (radius) of the ring at a random angle.
- **Visual Realism**: A random tilt of ±20° is applied to every dart for a more natural appearance.

### FOR THE CPU (STRATEGIC RESOLUTION)
The CPU uses an algorithm to decide its target:
1. **Ring Evaluation**: It calculates a "Desirability Score" for each ring by summing the point values of all uncompleted/unclosed numbers on that ring.
2. **Threshold**: If any ring has a score > 20, the CPU prioritizes hitting that ring for maximum efficiency.
3. **Fallback**: If no ring is highly desirable, it targets the highest remaining uncompleted number (e.g., #14, then #13).
4. **Processing**: Once a target is picked, it uses the same logic engine as human players to ensure fairness.

## 8. COMPLETION & CLOSURE LOGIC

### HIT TRACKING & SHARED BOARD COMPLETION
The game maintains hits for both players, but completion is **Shared**.
- `player.hits[n]++` is incremented.
- A number is **Closed** for the entire board when `PlayerA.hits[n] + PlayerB.hits[n] >= n`.
- **Logic**: No single player is mandatory to hit a dart number for the full count. The hits of **both players** count toward the shared target.

### SCORING UPON CLOSURE
Once the shared target is reached (Board Closed):
1. **Filler Points**: Distributed based on each player's individual hits (capped at their contribution).
2. **Top Filler Bonus (+7)**: Awarded to the player who landed the most hits (majority share). Split if tied.
3. **Fill-Up Bonus (+10)**: Awarded to the player who lands the **final (Nth)** hit that closes the number.

### EXAMPLES OF COMPLETION & CLOSURE

**Example 1: Majority Share (#10)**
- **Scenario**: Player A has 6 hits, Player B has 4 hits on #10.
- **Result**: Board Closes. Player A gets +7 Top Filler Bonus. Player B earns 4 hits worth of filler pts but no bonus.

**Example 2: Board Closure & Fill-Up Bonus (#3)**
- **Scenario**: Player A lands 1 hit on #3. Player B lands 1 hit on #3. Player A then lands a second hit on #3 (the "3rd" total board hit).
- **Logic**: Total board hits = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3. Since `3rd hit` closes #3, Board is now **Closed**.
- **Result**: Player A earns +2 pts for the hit AND **+10 pts Fill-Up Bonus** for being the one to close the board. Any subsequent hits on #3 by either player give 0 points.

**Example 3: Ring Impact on Completion**
- **Scenario**: Player A hits **Ring 1** (contains #14 and #13). Player A needed 1 more hit for #13 but 5 more for #14.
- **Logic**: Both numbers get +1 hit. 
- **Result**: Player A completes #13 (+2 filler pts) but #14 remains open (+2 filler pts).

**Example 4: Skipping a Closed Number**
- **Scenario**: #13 is Closed. Player B hits **Ring 1** (#14, #13).
- **Logic**: Game checks index for #13 in the closed set. It is found.
- **Result**: Player B gets +1 hit and +2 pts for #14. For #13, the hit is ignored entirely: 0 hits and 0 pts recorded.
